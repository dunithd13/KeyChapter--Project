Atomic Habits

by James Clear

  • Small daily improvements of just 1% compound over time, leading to remarkable long-term results.
  • Every habit follows a four-step loop: cue, craving, response, and reward, and understanding this helps build or break habits.
  • The Two-Minute Rule simplifies habits by encouraging you to start with actions that take two minutes or less.
  • Focusing on identity rather than outcomes—like becoming a "reader" instead of just finishing a book—makes habits more meaningful.
  • Environment design is crucial for habit formation; shaping your surroundings can make good habits easier and bad habits harder.
